    Effort.jl: a fast and differentiable emulator for the Effective Field Theory of the Large Scale Structure of the Universe

    This team has developed a novel and efficient emulator designed for the Effective Field Theory of Large-Scale Structure (EFTofLSS).

    Package: CosmologicalEmulators/Effort.jl

    Their implementation leverages state-of-the-art numerical methods and machine learning techniques from the SciML and Turing.jl ecosystems to create surrogate models of the large-scale structure of the Universe. These models integrate well with gradient-based samplers to achieve exceptional computational performance without sacrificing accuracy, which will be essential for analyzing next-generation cosmological survey data from, e.g., the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI), and Euclid.

    Read the 404 Media news release on this package, or read from an up-to-date list of associated publications from the CosmologicalEmulators organization here for more.

    3. H. Zhang, M. Bonici, A. Rocher, W. J. Percival, A. de Mattia, et al., (2025). Enhancing DESI DR1 Full-Shape analyses using HOD-informed priors. arXiv:2504.10407. [ADS]
